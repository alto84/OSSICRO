# Documents & Essential Records — Section Index

> [!authority] Governing authority
> ICH E6(R3) **Appendix C** ("Essential Records for the Conduct of a Clinical Trial", Step 4 adopted 2025-01) with legacy **ICH E6(R2) §8** cross-numbering; 21 CFR Parts **312, 50, 56, 54**; load-bearing FDA forms (1571, 1572, 3454/3455, 3500A). Status: **Mixed** — the catalog separates black-letter *confirmed* records from *conditional/interpretive* ones inline.

This section is the documentary spine of OSSICRO. Every accountable role ([[sponsor]], [[investigator]], [[sponsor-investigator]], [[irb-iec]], [[dsmb-dmc]], [[clinical-monitor-cra]], [[medical-monitor]]) discharges its obligations through records, and a trial's defensibility on FDA inspection ([[fda-as-counterparty]]) is, in practice, the completeness and integrity of those records. E6(R3) deliberately renamed "essential *documents*" to "essential *records*" to encompass dynamic and electronic content, and made the set **risk-proportionate**: the nature and extent of records depend on trial design and materiality, so not every record listed here is mandatory for every trial. The section catalogs each record, maps it to its governing authority, and organizes it into the three lifecycle checklists a clinician actually works through.

## The three checklists at a glance

The lifecycle split maps to [[phase-model-overview|the phase model]] and to E6(R3) Appendix C's before / during / after structure (which carries the E6(R2) §8.2 / §8.3 / §8.4 lineage):

- **STARTUP** ([[startup-tmf-checklist]]) — the records that gate first enrollment. The bright lines: no subject is screened before **documented IRB approval** ([[irb-submission-and-approval|56.109/56.115]]) and no drug is administered before the **IND is in effect** (312.40, 30-day clock). See [[site-activation]].
- **CONDUCT** ([[conduct-tmf-checklist]]) — the records generated by running the trial. The clocks live here: investigator SAE-to-sponsor (312.64(b)), sponsor **7-day / 15-day** IND safety reports (312.32), IRB continuing review, and the [[ind-annual-report-dsur|60-day annual report]] (312.33). See [[conduct-and-monitoring]] and [[safety-reporting-lifecycle]].
- **CLOSEOUT** ([[closeout-tmf-checklist]]) — the records that end the trial and start the retention clock. Drug reconciliation/destruction (312.59), FDA completion/withdrawal (312.38)/inactivation (312.45), final IRB notice, [[clinical-study-report|CSR]] (ICH E3), ClinicalTrials.gov results, and archival (312.57(c)/312.62(c)). See [[closeout]] and [[record-retention-and-archival]].

## The sponsor-investigator collapse

A defining feature of the [[sponsor-investigator]] model (21 CFR 312.3(b)) is that documents that normally flow *between* two entities collapse into self-directed obligations that still must be discharged. The **1572** the investigator gives the sponsor becomes a record the physician executes and retains for their own IND. The **IND safety reports** a sponsor sends to its investigators become reports the physician originates and files to FDA/IRB. The obligations do not disappear when the two roles merge into one person — a point the catalog flags on each affected record. See [[sponsor-cro-site-coordination]] and [[two-modes-site-vs-sponsor-investigator]].

## How OSSICRO uses this section

The catalog is not documentation-about-documentation; it is the **essential-records matrix** the [[generate-check-validate-engine|generate/check/validate engine]] validates against. The **check** pass tests a package for completeness against the risk-proportionate subset of this catalog; the [[completeness-ledger]] renders the result as green (validated) / amber (needs human judgment) / red (missing data + the exact resolving question). The [[verifiable-site-qualification-dossier]] is this catalog turned into a signed, citation-complete manifest for a skeptical [[pharma-partner-sponsor|pharma sponsor]].

> [!interpretive] OSSICRO position
> Every record OSSICRO produces is a **draft for qualified human review**, carrying a citation to the governing authority in this section and a gate routing any non-delegable act to a named human. OSSICRO drafts, checks, versions, routes, and times records; it never signs a 1571/1572, never *consents* a subject, never renders IRB approval, and never adjudicates SAE causality. See [[non-delegable-functions-and-gates]].

> [!warning] Non-delegable
> A complete TMF does not make a trial compliant — it *evidences* compliance that qualified humans produced. The judgments behind the records (the sponsor's "reasonably safe" conclusion, the IRB's approval, the investigator's eligibility and causality calls, the signatures on 1571/1572) remain human and are gated throughout this section.
