# IRB Submission and Approval

> [!authority] Governing authority
> 21 CFR Part 56 (Institutional Review Boards) — approval criteria at [21 CFR 56.111](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-56); investigator assurance at [21 CFR 312.66](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-D/part-312/subpart-D/section-312.66); consent standards at [21 CFR Part 50](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-50); 45 CFR 46 where federal funding attaches; ICH E6(R3) Annex 1 §1. Status: **Mixed** — the approval criteria and enrollment gate are confirmed black-letter requirements; OSSICRO's pre-submission completeness check is an interpretive design position.

IRB review is the ethics gate of the trial lifecycle. No investigator may involve a human subject in FDA-regulated research unless the clinical investigation has been reviewed and approved by an IRB meeting Part 56 requirements (21 CFR 56.103(a)), and the [[investigator]] must give the sponsor written assurance of that review (21 CFR 312.66). This page covers assembly of the initial submission package, the 56.111 approval criteria against which every package is judged, the review pathways, and how OSSICRO gates enrollment on documented approval. It sits between [[ind-submission-and-30-day-clock]] (the two run in parallel; neither substitutes for the other) and [[site-activation]].

## The legal gate

Two independent prohibitions converge here, and both are confirmed requirements:

1. **21 CFR 56.103(a):** a clinical investigation regulated by FDA shall not be initiated unless reviewed and approved by, and remains subject to continuing review by, an IRB meeting Part 56 requirements.
2. **21 CFR 312.66:** the investigator must assure that an IRB complying with Part 56 will be responsible for initial and continuing review and approval; must promptly report to the IRB all changes in the research activity and all unanticipated problems involving risk to human subjects or others; and must make no changes in the research without IRB approval, except where necessary to eliminate apparent immediate hazards to human subjects.

IRB approval and FDA's IND may-proceed are **separate, non-substitutable gates**. A study may hold an active IND and still be barred from enrolling for want of IRB approval, and vice versa. In the [[sponsor-investigator]] model both gates are the same physician's responsibility to clear; neither collapses.

> [!warning] Non-delegable
> The approval judgment itself — weighing risks against anticipated benefits, equitable selection, adequacy of consent — is the IRB's collective ethical determination, rendered by a convened quorum (or a designated reviewer under the expedited procedure of 21 CFR 56.110). It cannot be performed, predicted, or pre-empted by software, by the sponsor, or by the investigator. OSSICRO assembles and completeness-checks the submission; the IRB alone approves (see [[non-delegable-functions-and-gates]] and [[irb-iec]]).

## Assembling the initial submission package

The IRB must receive materials sufficient to apply the 56.111 criteria (21 CFR 56.108, 56.109(a); ICH E6(R3) Annex 1 §1). A complete initial package for an early-phase IND study typically comprises (see [[irb-submission-package]] for the per-item validator):

| Item | Basis |
|---|---|
| Protocol (and any amendments to date) | 21 CFR 312.23(a)(6); [[clinical-protocol-and-synopsis]] |
| [[investigators-brochure|Investigator's Brochure]] (or package insert per 312.55 for a marketed drug) | 21 CFR 312.23(a)(5), 312.55 |
| Draft informed consent form(s), assent forms, and any short-form documents | 21 CFR 50.25, 50.27; [[informed-consent-form]] |
| Recruitment and advertising materials directed at subjects | 21 CFR 56.111(a)(3)-(4); FDA information sheet on recruiting study subjects |
| Investigator CV and medical license | 21 CFR 312.53(c)(2) |
| [[form-fda-1572-statement-of-investigator|Form FDA 1572]] (or site/IRB identification consistent with it) | 21 CFR 312.53(c)(1) |
| Financial-interest information / conflict-of-interest disclosure | 21 CFR Part 54; [[form-fda-3454-3455-financial-disclosure]] |
| IND number and sponsor authorization (Mode A) or evidence the S-I holds the IND (Mode B) | 21 CFR 312.20, 312.40 |
| Data-and-safety-monitoring plan; [[dsmb-charter|DSMB charter]] where one is constituted | 21 CFR 56.111(a)(6); FDA DMC guidance (2006; 2024 draft) |
| HIPAA authorization language or waiver request | 45 CFR 164.508, 164.512(i)(1)(i); [[hipaa-and-privacy-gating]] |
| Site-specific context (facilities, population, local requirements) | 21 CFR 56.107(a) (local-context competence) |

Composition varies by IRB; central IRBs (WCG, Advarra) publish their own submission checklists, which OSSICRO encodes as institution-specific templates.

## The 21 CFR 56.111 approval criteria

The IRB shall approve only where **all** of the following are satisfied — this is the checklist the reviewing body applies and the rubric OSSICRO's pre-check mirrors:

1. **56.111(a)(1)** — Risks to subjects are minimized, by procedures consistent with sound research design that do not unnecessarily expose subjects to risk, and, whenever appropriate, by using procedures already being performed for diagnostic or treatment purposes.
2. **56.111(a)(2)** — Risks are reasonable in relation to anticipated benefits, if any, to subjects, and to the importance of the knowledge that may reasonably be expected to result. (The IRB does not weigh long-range public-policy effects.)
3. **56.111(a)(3)** — Selection of subjects is equitable, with attention to the purposes of the research, the setting, and the special problems of vulnerable populations.
4. **56.111(a)(4)** — Informed consent will be sought from each prospective subject or the subject's legally authorized representative, in accordance with and to the extent required by [21 CFR Part 50](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-50).
5. **56.111(a)(5)** — Informed consent will be appropriately documented, in accordance with and to the extent required by 21 CFR 50.27.
6. **56.111(a)(6)** — Where appropriate, the research plan makes adequate provision for monitoring the data collected to ensure the safety of subjects.
7. **56.111(a)(7)** — Where appropriate, there are adequate provisions to protect the privacy of subjects and maintain the confidentiality of data.
8. **56.111(b)** — Where some or all subjects are likely to be vulnerable to coercion or undue influence (children, prisoners, pregnant women, handicapped or mentally disabled persons, economically or educationally disadvantaged persons), additional safeguards are included. Pediatric studies additionally engage 21 CFR Part 50, Subpart D (50.50–50.56).

## Review pathways

- **Convened (full-board) review** is the default for initial review of a greater-than-minimal-risk IND study: majority of a quorum, including at least one nonscientist member present (21 CFR 56.107, 56.108(c)).
- **Expedited review** (21 CFR 56.110) is available only for research appearing on FDA's published list involving no more than minimal risk, and for minor changes to previously approved research — rarely available for initial review of an early-phase interventional drug study, routinely used for minor amendments.
- **Central / single IRB.** A [[sponsor-investigator]] without an institutional IRB typically engages a commercial central IRB. For cooperative research subject to the revised Common Rule, reliance on a single IRB is mandated (45 CFR 46.114(b), compliance date January 20, 2020; NIH sIRB policy NOT-OD-16-094); FDA regulations permit but do not compel central IRB use. See [[single-irb-mandate-and-central-irbs]] and [[irb-review-workflow]].

## Outcomes, documentation, and the enrollment gate

The IRB notifies the investigator and the institution in writing of its decision to approve, require modifications to secure approval, or disapprove (21 CFR 56.109(e)). The essential records generated — the approval letter with approval and expiration dates, the version-stamped approved protocol and consent form(s), and the IRB roster or statement of Part 56 compliance — are filed to the TMF/ISF ([[startup-tmf-checklist]], [[regulatory-binder-isf-index]]); the IRB itself keeps the records required by 21 CFR 56.115. **Enrollment is gated on the documented approval letter and on use of the exact IRB-approved consent version** — screening or consenting a subject before that letter exists, or on a superseded ICF version, is a reportable violation.

## Continuing review and changes

FDA requires continuing review at intervals appropriate to the degree of risk, **not less than once per year** (21 CFR 56.109(f)). Note the divergence for dual-regulated studies: the revised Common Rule eliminated mandatory annual continuing review for certain minimal-risk and analysis-only studies (45 CFR 46.109(f)(1)), but FDA's Part 56 requirement stands for FDA-regulated research. Changes to approved research require prior IRB approval except where necessary to eliminate apparent immediate hazards (21 CFR 56.108(a)(4), 312.66); see [[annual-reporting-and-amendments]]. Unanticipated problems involving risk to subjects are reported promptly to the IRB (21 CFR 56.108(b), 312.66) — the IRB arm of the [[safety-reporting-lifecycle]].

## OSSICRO's function at this gate

OSSICRO (i) assembles the submission package from the study record via the [[generate-check-validate-engine]]; (ii) runs a completeness-and-consistency pre-check that maps every package element to the 56.111 criterion it serves and flags gaps in the [[completeness-ledger]]; (iii) tracks the continuing-review clock and generates the progress-report package; and (iv) enforces the enrollment gate in code — the [[enrollment-and-consent]] workflow will not open until the approval letter and version-matched ICF are filed.

> [!interpretive] OSSICRO position
> The pre-submission check is a *completeness and internal-consistency screen against the 56.111 rubric* — it verifies that the package contains what an IRB needs to apply each criterion (e.g., a data-monitoring provision exists; consent contains every 50.25 element). It does not and cannot predict, score, or substitute for the IRB's ethical judgment on any criterion. Framing the check as "56.111 pre-check" is an OSSICRO design position, not a regulatory category.

> [!warning] Non-delegable
> Signing and submitting the package, responding to IRB stipulations, and attesting to the accuracy of its contents are the investigator's (or sponsor-investigator's) acts. OSSICRO drafts complete documentation for qualified human review; the human reviews, signs, and submits.
