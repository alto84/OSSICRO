# Clinical Trial Agreement — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [ICH E6(R3)](https://www.ich.org/page/efficacy-guidelines) Annex 1 (Sponsor responsibilities — a sponsor that engages a site or investigator must define responsibilities in a written agreement); [21 CFR 312.52](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.52) applies if regulatory obligations are transferred to a contract research organization (see the companion [[transfer-of-regulatory-obligations-toro]] template). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human — institutional counsel and authorized signatories — reviews and signs.

**Purpose.** The Clinical Trial Agreement (CTA) is the binding contract between the sponsor and the institution/investigator that allocates responsibilities, payment, publication rights, subject-injury coverage, indemnification, and record/inspection obligations for the conduct of a specific protocol. It is executed during study startup, before site activation and before any protocol-required procedure is performed at the site.

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. This template is a starting structure for negotiation, not a substitute for legal review. Every executed CTA must be reviewed by counsel for both parties and signed by persons with actual signature authority.

## Template

### CLINICAL TRIAL AGREEMENT

**Protocol No.:** {{protocol_number}}
**Protocol Title:** {{protocol_title}}
**Effective Date:** {{effective_date}}

This Clinical Trial Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

**Sponsor:** {{sponsor_legal_name}}, a {{sponsor_entity_type}} with a principal place of business at {{sponsor_address}} ("Sponsor"); and

**Institution:** {{institution_legal_name}}, with a principal place of business at {{institution_address}} ("Institution"); and

**Principal Investigator:** {{investigator_name}}, {{investigator_degrees}} ("Investigator").

[INSTRUCTION: The three-party structure above (sponsor / institution / investigator) is standard for institutional sites. For a private-practice site where the investigator's practice entity contracts directly, collapse Institution and Investigator into a single party and adjust all references. For a sponsor-investigator study with a pharma partner supplying drug, this document is usually replaced or supplemented by an investigator-initiated study (IIS) agreement — see [[pharma-partner-interface-iis]].]

Collectively, the "Parties": {{parties}}.

#### RECITALS

WHEREAS, Sponsor is conducting a clinical investigation under Protocol {{protocol_number}}, "{{protocol_title}}" (the "Protocol"), under IND {{ind_number}} [INSTRUCTION: delete IND reference if the study is not conducted under an IND, and record the exemption basis in the study record];

WHEREAS, Institution and Investigator have the qualifications, facilities, and patient population to conduct the Protocol;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows.

#### 1. SCOPE OF WORK

1.1 Investigator shall conduct the study in strict accordance with (a) the Protocol and all amendments approved by the reviewing IRB, (b) 21 CFR Parts 50, 54, 56, and 312, (c) ICH E6(R3) Good Clinical Practice, and (d) the terms of this Agreement.

1.2 Investigator shall not begin protocol-required procedures until: the reviewing IRB ({{irb_name}}) has approved the Protocol and consent documents; Investigator has signed Form FDA 1572 (if under an IND); and this Agreement is fully executed.

1.3 Enrollment target: {{enrollment_target}} subjects. [INSTRUCTION: state whether the target is a cap, a competitive-enrollment allowance, or a commitment, and any minimum-enrollment consequences.]

#### 2. TERM

2.1 This Agreement is effective as of {{effective_date}} and continues until study completion or termination under Section 11, whichever occurs first. Sections identified in Section 12.4 survive termination.

#### 3. REGULATORY RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 Sponsor retains all sponsor obligations under 21 CFR 312 Subpart D except any obligations transferred in writing under 21 CFR 312.52. [INSTRUCTION: if a CRO is engaged, attach or cross-reference the executed Transfer of Regulatory Obligations; do not restate transferred obligations inconsistently here.]

3.2 Investigator shall comply with all investigator obligations under 21 CFR 312 Subpart D (§§ 312.60–312.69), including informed consent (21 CFR Part 50), IRB review (21 CFR Part 56), investigational product control (21 CFR 312.61–312.62), records and reports (21 CFR 312.62, 312.64), and inspection access (21 CFR 312.68).

3.3 Financial disclosure: Investigator and each subinvestigator shall provide accurate financial disclosure information under 21 CFR Part 54 at study start and shall update it for one year following study completion.

3.4 Debarment: Each Party certifies that it has not used, and will not use, the services of any person debarred under 21 U.S.C. 335a in connection with this study.

#### 4. PAYMENT

4.1 Sponsor shall compensate Institution per the study budget attached as **Exhibit A** ({{budget_reference}}), which the Parties represent reflects fair market value for the work performed and was not determined in a manner that takes into account the volume or value of any referrals or other business between the Parties.

4.2 Payment schedule: {{payment_schedule}}. [INSTRUCTION: state the payee entity, remittance address/ACH details placeholder, invoicing vs. auto-payment mechanism, per-visit vs. milestone triggers, holdback percentage if any, and timing (e.g., net 45 after verified visit data entry).]

4.3 No payment under this Agreement is contingent on study outcome. Payments to physicians and teaching hospitals may be reportable by an applicable manufacturer under the Open Payments program, 42 CFR Part 403 Subpart I.

#### 5. INVESTIGATIONAL PRODUCT

5.1 Sponsor shall supply {{drug_name}} at no cost to Institution. Investigator shall maintain accountability records under 21 CFR 312.62(a), administer the product only to subjects under Investigator's or a subinvestigator's personal supervision (21 CFR 312.61), and return or destroy unused product per Sponsor's written instructions (21 CFR 312.59).

#### 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Each Party shall hold the other's Confidential Information in confidence for {{confidentiality_term}} years from disclosure, using it solely to perform this Agreement. Exclusions: information that is public without breach, independently developed, rightfully received from a third party, or required to be disclosed by law (with prompt notice to the disclosing Party where lawful).

6.2 Nothing in this Section limits Investigator's ability to communicate safety information to the IRB, FDA, or subjects, or to comply with ClinicalTrials.gov reporting obligations.

#### 7. PUBLICATION

7.1 Publication terms: {{publication_terms}}.

[INSTRUCTION: Complete with the negotiated position. A defensible baseline for an academic site: Investigator may publish study results after (a) providing Sponsor a copy of the proposed publication at least 30–60 days before submission, (b) delaying submission up to an additional 60–90 days at Sponsor's request solely to permit patent filings, and (c) removing Sponsor Confidential Information other than study results. In multicenter studies, site publication typically waits for the multicenter publication or 12–18 months after study completion, whichever is first. GCP-aligned practice and ICMJE authorship standards disfavor sponsor veto rights over results; flag any clause giving Sponsor final approval of scientific conclusions for human review.]

#### 8. SUBJECT INJURY

8.1 Sponsor shall {{subject_injury_terms}}. [INSTRUCTION: state who pays for diagnosis and treatment of study-related injury, whether payment is primary or secondary to insurance, and exclusions (e.g., injury caused by protocol non-compliance). This clause must be consistent with the compensation-for-injury language in the informed consent form (21 CFR 50.25(a)(6)) — reconcile the two documents before either is finalized.]

#### 9. INDEMNIFICATION AND INSURANCE

9.1 Indemnification: {{indemnification_terms}}. [INSTRUCTION: typical structure — Sponsor indemnifies Institution/Investigator for claims arising from the Protocol design, the investigational product, or Sponsor's use of study results; carve-outs for negligence, willful misconduct, and material Protocol deviation by Institution/Investigator. Institutional counsel must review; state institutions may be constrained by sovereign-immunity or anti-indemnity statutes.]

9.2 Each Party shall maintain insurance (or a program of self-insurance) adequate to cover its obligations, including for Sponsor clinical-trial liability coverage of not less than {{sponsor_insurance_amount}}.

#### 10. RECORDS, MONITORING, AND INSPECTION

10.1 Investigator shall retain study records for the period required by 21 CFR 312.62(c) — 2 years after marketing-application approval for the studied indication, or 2 years after the investigation is discontinued and FDA is notified — and shall not destroy records without giving Sponsor prior written notice.

10.2 Investigator shall permit monitoring and auditing by Sponsor or its designee and inspection by FDA and other regulatory authorities (21 CFR 312.68), with reasonable advance notice for Sponsor visits and prompt notice to Sponsor of any regulatory inspection.

#### 11. TERMINATION

11.1 Either Party may terminate for material breach on {{cure_period_days}} days' written notice if the breach is not cured. Sponsor may terminate immediately for subject-safety reasons, IRB or FDA action, or clinical hold. Investigator may withdraw for cause with reasonable notice, subject to safe transition of enrolled subjects.

11.2 On termination, Institution is paid for work properly performed and non-cancelable obligations through the termination date; unspent advance payments are refunded.

#### 12. GENERAL

12.1 Governing law: {{governing_law}}. 12.2 Notices to the addresses above. 12.3 No assignment without written consent. 12.4 Survival: Sections 3.3, 4.3, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, and 12. 12.5 This Agreement, with Exhibits, is the entire agreement and supersedes prior understandings on its subject matter.

**Exhibit A:** Study Budget ({{budget_reference}}) — see [[study-budget-fmv]].
**Exhibit B:** Protocol {{protocol_number}} (incorporated by reference).
