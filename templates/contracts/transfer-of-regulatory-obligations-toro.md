# Transfer of Regulatory Obligations (TORO) — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [21 CFR 312.52(a)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.52) — a sponsor may transfer any or all of its obligations to a contract research organization; any such transfer **must be described in writing**; if not all obligations are transferred, the writing must describe **each specific obligation** transferred (a general statement is acceptable only for a transfer of *all* obligations). [21 CFR 312.52(b)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.52) — a CRO that assumes an obligation is subject to the same regulatory action as a sponsor for failure to comply, and all references to "sponsor" in 21 CFR Part 312 apply to the CRO to the extent of the assumed obligations. This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human — authorized officers of the sponsor and the CRO — reviews and signs.

**Purpose.** The TORO is the written instrument by which a sponsor (or sponsor-investigator) transfers specific regulatory obligations under 21 CFR Part 312 to a contract research organization. It is executed at startup (or when a CRO is engaged mid-study) and documented to FDA — typically by inclusion or reference in the IND (the 1571 identifies transferred obligations) and retention in the TMF.

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. The obligation table is the legally operative core: mark every row explicitly as transferred or retained. Obligations not listed as transferred remain with the sponsor by operation of 21 CFR 312.52(a).

#### 3. CRO Acknowledgment (21 CFR 312.52(b))

3.1 CRO acknowledges that, for each obligation it assumes under this instrument, CRO is subject to the same regulatory action as a sponsor for failure to comply, and that all references to "sponsor" in 21 CFR Part 312 apply to CRO to the extent of the assumed obligations.

3.2 CRO shall perform the assumed obligations in compliance with 21 CFR Part 312, ICH E6(R3), the Protocol, and applicable law, using qualified personnel, and shall promptly notify Sponsor of any circumstance materially affecting its ability to perform.

#### 4. Retained Authority and Oversight

4.1 Notwithstanding any transfer, Sponsor retains ultimate responsibility for the quality and integrity of the trial data and shall exercise appropriate oversight of CRO-performed activities (ICH E6(R3) Annex 1). Transfer of an obligation does not eliminate the need for sponsor oversight of the CRO.

4.2 Sponsor retains the right to audit CRO's performance of the assumed obligations on reasonable notice.

#### 5. Documentation to FDA

5.1 Sponsor shall identify the transfer on Form FDA 1571 (item identifying transferred obligations and the CRO's name and address) and shall submit or reference this instrument in the IND as required.
