# Informed Consent Form (21 CFR Part 50) — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [21 CFR 50.25 — Elements of informed consent](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/50.25); [21 CFR 50.27 — Documentation of informed consent](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/50.27); [21 CFR 312.60 — General responsibilities of investigators](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.60). See also [21 CFR 50.20 — General requirements for informed consent](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/50.20) and [FDA Informed Consent Guidance (2023)](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/informed-consent). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human reviews and signs, and no subject may be enrolled until the IRB has approved this document (21 CFR 56.109, 312.66).

**Purpose.** The informed consent form is the written document by which a prospective subject (or legally authorized representative) is given the information required by 21 CFR 50.25 and documents legally effective consent under 21 CFR 50.27 before any study-specific procedure is performed. The investigator is personally responsible for obtaining consent (21 CFR 312.60).

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. Target an 8th-grade reading level; write in second person ("you"); avoid exculpatory language — no language through which the subject waives or appears to waive any legal rights or releases the investigator, sponsor, or institution from liability for negligence (21 CFR 50.20). The IRB-approved, version-controlled copy is the only copy that may be used for consent.

### 1. Invitation and Statement of Research — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(1)

You are being asked to take part in a research study. This form explains the study so you can decide whether to participate. Take the time you need, ask any questions, and discuss it with family, friends, or your own doctor before deciding.

**Why is this study being done?**

{{purpose}}

**What will happen during the study?**

{{procedures}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe the procedures chronologically (screening, treatment, follow-up), the number and length of visits, any randomization ("like flipping a coin"), blinding, and washout. ### 2. Risks and Discomforts — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(2)

{{risks}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe reasonably foreseeable risks and discomforts of the investigational product and of study procedures, ordered by severity and likelihood, consistent with the current Investigator's Brochure and protocol. Include reproductive risks and contraception requirements if applicable (this also supports the 50.25(b)(1) additional element for unforeseeable risks to an embryo or fetus). Do not minimize.]

There may also be risks that are currently unknown or unforeseeable. [INSTRUCTION: If the treatment or procedure may involve risks to the subject (or to the embryo or fetus, if the subject is or may become pregnant) which are currently unforeseeable, this statement is the 50.25(b)(1) additional element — retain it.]

{{benefits}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe any benefits to the subject or to others that may reasonably be expected. If direct benefit is not expected, say so plainly. Payment for participation is NOT a benefit and must not be listed here.]

### 4. Alternatives to Participation — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(4)

{{alternatives}}

[INSTRUCTION: Disclose appropriate alternative procedures or courses of treatment, if any, that might be advantageous to the subject — including approved therapies, other trials, supportive/palliative care, and the option of no treatment.]

You do not have to join this study to receive care. Your other choices include the options described above.

### 5. Confidentiality — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(5)

{{confidentiality_statement}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe the extent to which confidentiality of records identifying the subject will be maintained: coding of study records, who may access identifiable records (sponsor {{sponsor_name}}, its representatives/CRO, the IRB, and regulatory authorities), and data storage/retention. The following FDA-inspection sentence is required by 50.25(a)(5) — retain it.]

Records that identify you may be inspected by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), and complete confidentiality cannot be guaranteed.

[INSTRUCTION: For any "applicable clinical trial" registered under 42 U.S.C. 282(j), the following statement is REQUIRED VERBATIM by 21 CFR 50.25(c) — do not edit its wording:]

*A description of this clinical trial will be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov, as required by U.S. Law. This Web site will not include information that can identify you. At most, the Web site will include a summary of the results. You can search this Web site at any time.*

### 6. In Case of Injury — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(6)

[INSTRUCTION: Required for research involving more than minimal risk. State whether any compensation is available and whether any medical treatments are available if injury occurs, what they consist of, and where further information may be obtained. Do NOT use exculpatory language; do not state or imply that the subject gives up the right to seek compensation.]

{{injury_compensation_statement}}

If you believe you have been injured as a result of taking part in this study, contact {{injury_contact_name}} at {{injury_contact_phone}} right away.

### 7. Costs and Payment

[INSTRUCTION: Not an enumerated 50.25(a) element, but expected by IRBs and by FDA guidance. State which items the study pays for (study drug, study-specific procedures), which may be billed to the subject or insurance, and any payment to subjects. If subjects are paid, describe amount, schedule, and proration; per 50.25(b)(6), any additional costs to the subject that may result from participation must be disclosed.]

{{costs_and_payment_statement}}

### 8. Questions and Contacts — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(7)

- **Questions about the research:** contact {{research_contact}}.
- **Questions about your rights as a research subject:** contact {{irb_name}} at {{irb_phone}}.
- **Research-related injury:** contact {{injury_contact_name}} at {{injury_contact_phone}}.

[INSTRUCTION: The rights contact must be someone other than the study team; the IRB or an institutional subject advocate satisfies this.]

### 9. Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal — 21 CFR 50.25(a)(8)

Taking part in this study is voluntary. You may refuse to participate, and you may stop at any time. Your decision will involve no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled, and it will not affect your regular medical care or your relationship with {{site_name}} or your doctors.

[INSTRUCTION: Describe the procedure for orderly withdrawal, whether a final safety visit is requested, and what happens to data already collected. If applicable, include the 50.25(b) additional elements below.]

- **(b)(3) Additional costs:** {{additional_costs_statement}}

I have read this consent form (or it has been read to me). I have had the opportunity to ask questions, and my questions have been answered to my satisfaction. I voluntarily agree to take part in this study. I will receive a signed and dated copy of this form. I am not giving up any of my legal rights by signing this form.

> [!warning] Non-delegable
> The **Informed consent event** is executed by the **investigator** (21 CFR 50.20, 50.25, 50.27; 21 CFR 312.60). OSSICRO drafts the consent FORM; the consent EVENT — disclosure, comprehension, voluntary agreement, signature — is a human act between a qualified clinician and the subject. The engine cannot finalize this document without a recorded human sign-off, and no version may be used before IRB approval.
