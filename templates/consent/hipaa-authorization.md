# HIPAA Authorization for Research — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [45 CFR 164.508 — Uses and disclosures for which an authorization is required](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/45/164.508). See also [OCR/HHS guidance on research authorizations](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/special-topics/research/index.html) and, for the interplay with FDA consent, [21 CFR 50.25](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/50.25). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human reviews it, the covered entity's privacy office confirms it against institutional requirements, and the subject signs.

**Purpose.** This authorization permits a HIPAA covered entity (the study site/provider) to use and disclose a subject's protected health information (PHI) for the research study, as required by 45 CFR 164.508 before research uses and disclosures of identifiable PHI. It is signed at the same consent event as the informed consent form and may, per institutional practice, be a stand-alone document or a compound authorization combined with the ICF (compound consent/authorization for the same research study is permitted under 45 CFR 164.508(b)(3)(i)).

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. An authorization missing any core element or required statement of 164.508(c), or with a known-false expiration, is DEFECTIVE and invalid under 164.508(b)(2) — every section below marked "required" must survive review. Plain language is required (164.508(c)(3)); a signed copy must be given to the individual (164.508(c)(4)).

### 1. What health information will be used or disclosed — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(1)(i)

If I sign this form, the following information about me may be used and shared for this research study:

{{phi_described}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe the PHI in a specific and meaningful fashion, e.g., "medical history, physical examination findings, laboratory and imaging results, medication records, and study visit records created between screening and end of study; portions of my existing medical record relevant to eligibility, including {{record_categories}}." Blanket phrases like "my entire medical record" require justification to the privacy office. If the study collects any of the following, name them explicitly, since state or federal law may require specific consent: HIV/AIDS status, mental/behavioral health or psychotherapy notes (psychotherapy notes require a SEPARATE authorization under 164.508(a)(2) — do not combine), substance use disorder records (42 CFR Part 2), and genetic information.]

### 2. Who may use or disclose the information — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(1)(ii)

{{site_name}}, the Principal Investigator {{investigator_name}}, and the study team at this site may use my information and share it with the people and organizations listed in Section 3.

### 3. Who may receive the information — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(1)(iii)

My information may be shared with:

{{recipients}}

[INSTRUCTION: List each class of recipient specifically, typically: the sponsor {{sponsor_name}} and its representatives (contract research organization, monitors, auditors); the reviewing IRB {{irb_name}}; the U.S. Food and Drug Administration and other U.S. or foreign regulatory agencies; the Office for Human Research Protections, if applicable; central laboratories, reading centers, and data-coordinating centers named in the protocol; and the data and safety monitoring board, if any.]

**Once my information is disclosed to a recipient that is not required to follow federal privacy law (HIPAA), it may be re-disclosed by that recipient and may no longer be protected by HIPAA.** [INSTRUCTION: Required statement, 45 CFR 164.508(c)(2)(iii) — retain.]

### 4. Purpose — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(1)(iv)

My information will be used and shared to conduct the research study named above, to evaluate its results, to check that the study was done correctly, and to meet legal and regulatory requirements related to the study.

{{purpose_statement}}

[INSTRUCTION: Describe each purpose specifically. "Future unspecified research" is not a valid purpose for this authorization unless the description is such that it would be reasonable for the individual to expect the PHI could be used for that future research (per OCR guidance); if future-research use of data/specimens is intended, describe it here or use a separate authorization.]

### 5. Expiration — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(1)(v)

This authorization is valid until {{expiration}}.

[INSTRUCTION: State an expiration date or expiration event that relates to the individual or the purpose. For research, "the end of the research study" or "none" is permissible — if "none" is used, say so explicitly, e.g., "This authorization does not expire," and explain why (e.g., research databases/repositories).]

### 6. Right to revoke — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(2)(i) and 164.508(b)(5)

{{revocation_statement}}

[INSTRUCTION: The statement must cover the right to revoke in writing, the exceptions, and how to revoke (or reference the covered entity's Notice of Privacy Practices if it describes the procedure). Model language:]

I may cancel (revoke) this authorization at any time by writing to: {{revocation_contact}}. If I revoke this authorization, the study team will stop collecting new information about me, except information already in the study record and information the study team needs to report about my safety or to preserve the integrity of the study, to the extent the site or investigator has already acted in reliance on this authorization (45 CFR 164.508(b)(5)(i)). Information used or shared before my revocation may continue to be used as described above.

### 7. Conditioning of treatment or participation — 45 CFR 164.508(c)(2)(ii)

[INSTRUCTION: For research-related treatment, a covered entity MAY condition study participation and research-related treatment on signing (164.508(b)(4)(i)). State the consequence honestly:]

I do not have to sign this authorization. My decision will not affect my regular (non-study) medical care, treatment, payment, enrollment, or eligibility for benefits outside this study. However, if I do not sign, I **cannot take part in this research study**, because the study team must be able to use my health information to conduct the study.

### 8. Other terms

- I will receive a signed copy of this authorization (45 CFR 164.508(c)(4)).
- I may not be able to see the study record while the research is in progress; my right of access may be suspended until the study is complete (45 CFR 164.524(a)(2)(iii)), at which point it resumes. [INSTRUCTION: Include only if the site elects the clinical-trial access suspension; if included, the individual must be informed here and agree.]
- {{additional_state_law_terms}} [INSTRUCTION: Insert any state-law-required disclosures (e.g., genetic privacy, HIV, mental health statutes) identified by the privacy office; delete if none.]

> [!warning] Non-delegable
> The **Informed consent event** is executed by the **investigator** (21 CFR 50.20, 50.25, 50.27; 21 CFR 312.60). This authorization is obtained as part of that human consent event: OSSICRO drafts and pre-populates the form, but the disclosure conversation and the subject's (or personal representative's) signature are human acts. The engine cannot finalize this document without a recorded human sign-off, and the covered entity's privacy office must approve the form before use.
