# FDA Study Completion / IND Withdrawal Notice — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [21 CFR 312.38](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.38) (withdrawal of an IND: withdrawal without prejudice; ending of all clinical investigations; drug disposition; safety-reason notifications). Related provisions used in this notice: [21 CFR 312.59](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.59) (disposition of unused supply of investigational drug), [21 CFR 312.57(c)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.57) (sponsor record retention after discontinuation), [21 CFR 312.45](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.45) (inactive status, the alternative to withdrawal), and [21 CFR 312.23(e)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.23) / [Form FDA 1571](https://www.fda.gov/media/72335/download) (every submission to the IND, including this notice, goes in under a serially numbered 1571 cover). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human reviews and signs.

**Purpose.** This notice tells FDA that the clinical investigation(s) under the IND are complete and — if the sponsor-investigator so elects — withdraws the IND under 21 CFR 312.38. Withdrawal is voluntary and without prejudice, but it carries mandatory consequences: all clinical investigations conducted under the IND must be ended, all current investigators notified, and all stocks of the drug returned to the sponsor or otherwise disposed of per 21 CFR 312.59. If the withdrawal is for safety reasons, 312.38(c) additionally requires prompt notification of FDA, all participating investigators, and all reviewing IRBs, with the reasons.
