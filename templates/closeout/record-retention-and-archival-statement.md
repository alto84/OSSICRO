# Record Retention and Archival Statement — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [21 CFR 312.57(c)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.57) (sponsor retention: 2 years after a marketing application is approved for the drug; or, if no application is approved, until 2 years after shipment and delivery of the drug for investigational use is discontinued and FDA has been so notified) and [21 CFR 312.62(c)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.62) (investigator retention: 2 years following the date a marketing application is approved for the drug for the indication for which it is being investigated; or, if no application is to be filed or if the application is not approved for such indication, until 2 years after the investigation is discontinued and FDA is notified). See also [ICH E6(R3)](https://www.ich.org/page/efficacy-guidelines) (essential records; sponsor informs the investigator/institution when trial records are no longer needed) and [21 CFR 312.58](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.58) / [312.68](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.68) (FDA inspection of sponsor and investigator records). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human reviews and signs.

**Purpose.** This statement is filed in the investigator site file (regulatory binder) and the trial master file at closeout. It fixes, in writing: what records are retained, for how long, where, in what format, under whose custody, and under what conditions they may be moved or destroyed — so that a site contacted years later (or an FDA investigator under 312.68) can locate intact, legible records.

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. In a sponsor-investigator (Mode B) study the same person carries both the 312.57(c) sponsor duty and the 312.62(c) investigator duty; state both and apply the LONGEST applicable period. [INSTRUCTION: The two-year CFR minimums are floors, not targets. Check every overlay before setting {{retention_period}}: institutional policy, the clinical trial agreement, HIPAA (45 CFR 164.530(j): 6 years for authorizations and related documentation), state medical-records law, and — if the data may ever support an EU filing — EU CTR Regulation 536/2014 Art. 58 (25 years). The longest wins.]

### 1. Records covered

This statement covers all records required to be maintained under 21 CFR Part 312 and the essential records of ICH E6(R3) Appendix C, including without limitation: case histories and source documents (21 CFR 312.62(b)), signed informed consent documents (21 CFR 50.27), investigational product disposition records (21 CFR 312.62(a), 312.57(a)), the subject identification code list, all versions of the protocol and investigator's brochure, IRB correspondence and approvals, Forms FDA 1571/1572 and financial disclosure records (21 CFR 54.6), monitoring documentation, safety reports, and the delegation and signature logs. {{records_scope_additions}} [INSTRUCTION: Add any study-specific record classes — imaging, biospecimen chain-of-custody, ePRO exports, EDC audit trails — or enter "None."]

### 2. Retention period

Records will be retained for: **{{retention_period}}**, measured from the trigger in Section 3.

Regulatory floor applied:

- **Investigator records (21 CFR 312.62(c)):** 2 years following the date a marketing application is approved for {{drug_name}} for the indication under investigation; or, if no application is to be filed or the application is not approved for that indication, until 2 years after the investigation is discontinued and FDA is notified.
- **Sponsor records (21 CFR 312.57(c)):** 2 years after a marketing application is approved for {{drug_name}}; or, if no application is approved, until 2 years after shipment and delivery of the drug for investigational use is discontinued and FDA has been so notified.
- **Overlays applied ({{overlay_authorities}}):** {{overlay_requirements}} [INSTRUCTION: List each longer requirement actually applicable (institutional SOP, CTA clause, 45 CFR 164.530(j), state law, EU CTR) and its period; the stated {{retention_period}} must equal or exceed the longest. Enter "None beyond CFR minimums" only after checking.]

### 3. Retention trigger and clock status

The retention clock starts on: {{retention_trigger}}. [INSTRUCTION: One of: (a) marketing-application approval date for the studied indication; (b) the date the investigation was discontinued AND FDA was notified — cite the Study Completion / IND Withdrawal Notice serial number and date; (c) a defined contractual date. Note that the 312.62(c)/312.57(c) alternative prong does not begin until FDA notification actually occurs; record that notification here: {{fda_notification_reference}}.]

As of the effective date of this statement, the clock status is: {{clock_status}}. [INSTRUCTION: E.g., "Running — FDA notified of study completion on {{fda_notification_reference}}; earliest permissible destruction date {{earliest_destruction_date}}" or "Not yet running — marketing application decision pending; records must be held indefinitely until a trigger occurs."]

**No record covered by this statement may be destroyed before {{earliest_destruction_date}}, and then only under Section 6.**

### 4. Archive location and format

- **Electronic archive:** {{electronic_archive_location}} [INSTRUCTION: System name, media, and where certified copies and EDC/eTMF exports (with audit trails and metadata) reside. - **Format:** {{archive_format}} [INSTRUCTION: Original paper / certified copies / hybrid. If originals were converted to certified copies, reference the certification procedure per ICH E6(R3).]
- **Storage conditions:** {{storage_conditions}} [INSTRUCTION: Fire protection, environmental controls, restricted access, and — for records containing subject identifiers — the confidentiality safeguards promised in the ICF and HIPAA authorization.]

### 5. Records custodian

- **Custodian:** {{custodian_name}}, {{custodian_title}}
- **Contact:** {{custodian_contact}}
- **Successor provision:** If the custodian leaves the institution or the investigator's practice, custody transfers to {{successor_custodian_provision}}, and the sponsor(-investigator) will be notified in writing within {{custody_change_notice_days}} days. [INSTRUCTION: 21 CFR 312.62(c) permits an investigator to transfer custody of records to another person if FDA-required access is preserved and the sponsor is notified; ICH E6(R3) requires the investigator/institution to inform the sponsor of any change in location, transfer, or ownership of records.]

### 6. Transfer and destruction controls

1. Records may be transferred (to a successor investigator, the institution, the sponsor, or an archival vendor) only with written notice retained in the site file, preserving FDA's access under 21 CFR 312.68.
2. No record will be destroyed before {{earliest_destruction_date}}, and destruction additionally requires the sponsor(-investigator)'s prior written authorization confirming that no marketing application, regulatory inspection, or legal hold requires continued retention. [INSTRUCTION: In multi-party studies this mirrors ICH E6(R3): the sponsor informs the investigator/institution in writing when records are no longer needed. In a Mode B study, the sponsor-investigator issues that written determination for the site file.]
3. Destruction, when authorized, will be by {{destruction_method}} with a destruction certificate filed at {{destruction_certificate_location}}.
4. Any legal hold, FDA inspection notice, or pending audit suspends this section until released in writing.
