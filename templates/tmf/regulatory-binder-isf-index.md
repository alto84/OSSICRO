# Regulatory Binder / Investigator Site File Index — TEMPLATE (DRAFT for qualified human review)

> [!authority] Governing authority
> [ICH E6(R3) Appendix C — Essential Records for the Conduct of a Clinical Trial](https://www.ich.org/page/efficacy-guidelines) (Step 4 final guideline, January 2025; Appendix C replaces the E6(R2) Section 8 essential-documents list). Investigator record-control obligations also flow from [21 CFR 312.62](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.62) (investigator recordkeeping and record retention) and [21 CFR 312.68](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/312.68) (inspection of investigator's records and reports). This is a DRAFT template; OSSICRO fills it, a qualified human reviews and signs.

**Purpose.** The Investigator Site File (ISF, "regulatory binder") index is the site's master filing plan for the essential records that individually and collectively permit evaluation of the conduct of the trial and the quality of the data produced (ICH E6(R3) Appendix C). It is created at site activation, kept current throughout conduct, and is the first artifact a monitor, auditor, or FDA investigator uses to navigate the site's records.

**How to use.** Fill every {{placeholder}}; follow each [INSTRUCTION: ...] note; delete guidance text before finalizing. Tabs listed below are the reference set for a single-site sponsor-investigator IND study; add, remove, or merge tabs to match the actual trial, but record every change in the version history so the filing plan and the binder never diverge. Where a record is filed electronically or in another system (eISF, pharmacy, EDC), say so in the "Location" column rather than leaving the tab empty.
