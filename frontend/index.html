Generate · Check · Validate · Coordinate

Complete documentation, engineered for one-pass human review

01 · MATCH

Find the trial

Trial / condition / medication matching over ClinicalTrials.gov, PubMed & openFDA — three-valued eligibility (met / not-met / needs-data), recall-first, with a cited rationale. Eligibility is the investigator’s call.

02 · GENERATE

Draft the package

Fill the essential-document set from structured (and chart / SMART-on-FHIR) data. Every field carries provenance: source datum → governing citation.

03 · CHECK

Prove it’s complete

A completeness ledger — green / amber / red — plus cross-document consistency. No hidden blanks; every gap ships with the exact question to resolve.

04 · COORDINATE

Route to the right humans

IRB, DSMB, medical monitor, sponsor, FDA. Non-delegable gates route to the accountable person; the audit trail records who signed what, against which authority.

The scenario that drives the design

One physician. One patient. One investigational drug.

A neurologist has a patient with refractory epilepsy and a candidate early-phase therapy. Today, becoming a site is a 9-month, five-figure paperwork wall. OSSICRO routes the request to the right path — a one-patient site on an existing protocol, single-patient expanded access (Form 3926), or a sponsor-investigator IND — and drafts every document the pathway requires, with the pharma-side acceptance package on the other end.

Why this is lawful

Software can never be a CRO: 21 CFR 312.52 transfers sponsor obligations only to a legally accountable entity. So OSSICRO doesn’t replace the CRO — it lets a physician lawfully hold both roles as a sponsor-investigator (21 CFR 312.3(b)) and absorbs the coordination labor, with a thin accountable Micro-CRO for the irreducible functions.

The hard line

OSSICRO drafts complete documentation. It does not replace judgment.

It never performs a non-delegable act — informed consent, IRB / ethics-board review, DSMB oversight, SAE causality, statistical sign-off, or the 1571 / 1572 signatures. Those are legally reserved to qualified humans and oversight bodies. The gates are enforced in code: a gated document cannot be finalized without a recorded human sign-off. Every artifact is a draft for review. Not medical, legal, or regulatory advice.

Verifiable & cited

Every requirement maps to its governing authority; every draft span to its source. A hash-chained, Part-11 audit trail a sponsor can verify without trusting us.

Open source

MIT-licensed wiki, templates, and engine. A maintained, citation-linked knowledge base with a regulatory-change watch — so it doesn’t rot as E6(R3), M11, and the DCT guidance move.

Built for low-resource sites

Local-first, no PHI egress, single-container deployable — so the physicians who most need to enroll patients can, wherever they are.
